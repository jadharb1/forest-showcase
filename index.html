The Problem

Five critical failures
on 0.8 hectares of campus land

🌲
Monoculture Canopy

Pinus brutia plantation creates zero plant diversity at ground level. No understory, no shrubs, no life below 15m.

🧪
Acidic Compacted Soil

pH ~5.5 from decades of pine needle litter. Suppresses ALL other plant germination. Nothing can grow.

💧
Zero Water Harvesting

860mm of annual rainfall hits compacted soil and runs straight into Bliss St. Every drop is wasted.

🚫
No Food Production

0.8 Ha of prime campus land produces zero food for the AUB community. Completely underutilized.

🦋
Zero Biodiversity

No pollinators, no wildlife habitat, no educational value. An ecological dead zone despite being a living campus.

⚡
The Constraint

AUB policy: not a single tree can be cut. All design must work WITH the existing trees, between trunks, under canopy.

"From dead ground under 15-metre pines to a 7-layer food forest — fruit trees, shrubs, herbs, ground cover, climbers and mushrooms — all growing together under an untouched pine canopy."

Design Zones

Four zones.
One working ecosystem.

1

Herb Garden + Student Seating

Closest to the entrance — daily student interaction zone

  • Herb spiral (rosemary, sage, thyme, mint) — raised mound, no deep digging
  • Salad picking garden — raised beds fitted between tree roots
  • Medicinal herb beds: lavender, chamomile
  • Student seating/chill area under existing pine shade
  • Labeled plant trail for educational use in LDEM and FAFS classes
  • Sheet mulching with cardboard + wood chips builds soil over roots
🎓 Student benefit: Pick fresh herbs daily. Free herbs for AUB cafeteria. Aromatic walking paths. Stress-relief garden under the pines.
2

Food Forest Understory

Fruit trees planted between existing pines — zero removal

  • Fruit trees in canopy gaps: Fig, Almond, Pomegranate, Carob
  • Shrub layer: Elderberry, Bay Laurel, Rosemary
  • Ground cover: Strawberry, Nasturtium, Thyme
  • Oyster mushroom logs on already-fallen dead pine wood
  • Grape and jasmine climbers on existing pine trunks
  • No machinery needed — no roots disturbed
🍎 Student benefit: Fresh figs, almonds, pomegranates for the AUB community. Mushrooms grown at zero cost. Lebanese native food plants preserved.
3

Swales + Pollinator Meadow

860mm of rain stays on campus instead of running into Bliss St

  • 3 swales dug on contour — hand-dug between tree roots, no machinery
  • Underground cistern stores water for Jun–Sep dry season
  • Drip irrigation connects to Zone 1 and Zone 2
  • Native wildflower pollinator strip along swale edges
  • Sea buckthorn nitrogen fixer for soil improvement
  • Zero tree disturbance — all earthworks between root systems
💧 Student benefit: AUB campus retains its own rainwater. Students learn water harvesting principles directly on site. Bees and butterflies return to campus.
4

Wildlife Habitat + Composting

Deepest zone — least human interference, maximum ecological value

  • Hot composting station — AUB cafeteria food waste (zero cost feedstock)
  • 5 bird nesting boxes bolted to existing pine trunks
  • Seed saving area for native Lebanese plants
  • Wildlife corridor — bees, birds, beneficial insects
  • Comfrey dynamic accumulator — free fertilizer, chop-and-drop
  • Leaf litter kept as natural mulch — no removal needed
♻️ Student benefit: AUB cafeteria waste becomes free soil amendment for all zones. Native Lebanese seeds preserved. Biodiversity monitoring as outdoor lab.

Implementation

From compacted soil
to food forest — in 4 phases

Phase 1 · 0–6 months

Soil Remediation

Apply agricultural lime to raise pH from 5.5 toward 7. Layer AUB cafeteria compost. Sheet mulch with cardboard + wood chips. Inoculate with mycorrhizal fungi. No plants yet — let the soil heal first.

Phase 2 · 6–18 months

Water Infrastructure

Hand-dig 3 contour swales in Zone 3 between tree roots. Install underground cistern in open area. Lay drip irrigation lines to Zones 1 and 2. Build student walking paths from reclaimed wood.

Phase 3 · Year 2

Main Planting

Plant 15 fruit and nut trees in canopy gaps. Establish herb spiral and raised salad beds in Zone 1. Install native wildflower pollinator strip. Introduce shrub and ground cover layers throughout.

Phase 4 · Year 3+

Activation

Inoculate mushroom logs with oyster spawn. Launch student volunteer programs and harvest events. Begin outdoor LDEM and FAFS classes on site. Establish seed saving programme.

Year 10+

Self-Sustaining Food Forest

Full canopy closure. The 7-layer food forest is producing figs, almonds, pomegranates, carobs, herbs, mushrooms and wildflowers year-round — fed by its own water, its own compost, its own nutrient cycles.
